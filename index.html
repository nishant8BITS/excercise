Kilbronogue trip

by Matea Leka

“They lived and loved and laughed and left.”

James Joyce, Finnegans Wake

This is me.

I arrived in Ireland one foggy summer day and became pro-selfisolation survivor without ever catching corona. I went to Kilbronogue and here are my tips and tricks how to survive the peacefullness of one irish, pretty secluded village and why you should visit West Cork at the first place.

Promise yourself to go on a diet and then start to bake

Brownies, blondies, apple pies, banana bread... Each cake found its place in Kilbronogue's oven.

Learn a new card game and find a lousy oponent

Boosting your self-esteem is really important; especially if it happens you're better in Gin Rummy and Rumikub as well :)

Don't jump in the Atlantic ocean.
Unless you're Irish

Fascinated by Atlantic ocean, its tides and currents, the temperature will always stay cold for this warm slavic blood.

Climb the hill, avoid the sheep

According to this data rural population (% of total population) in Ireland was reported at 36.6% in 2019 and Ireland itself had 3.73 million sheep in 2018.

Walk old paths and picture Ireland before

Ireland is full of lovely, old paths built to connect villages mutually or with towns. The Butteroad path still connects Ballydehob and Schull.

Make friends with animals

Because it's necessary and because you need it.

Make fun of people who are trying to excercise.

Actually, don't do that.

Start learning Norwegian after reading about Jotunheimen Nasjonalpark

Plant some potatoes and be proud when you see them grow

potet - "potato" in Norwegian
